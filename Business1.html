Definitions

Business

The term business refer to any activity that is carried out by an individual or organization with an aim of making a profit.

Business studies

This is the study of the activities that are carried out in and around production, distribution and consumption of goods and services.

Goods

These are items or things that are tangible in that they can be touched and felt.

Services

These are items or things that are tangible in that they can be touched and felt.

Production

This is the Creation of goods and services or increasing their usefulness through activities such as transporting them to where it is required. People who produce goods and services are called producers.

Distribution

This is the movement of goods and services from producers to the users. Activities taking place during distribution.

  • Transportation
  • Storage
  • Insurance.
  • Communications
  • Buying and selling
  • Banking
  • Advertising

Consumption

This is the usage of goods and services. Those who use the goods are called consumers.

Economics

This is the study of how human beings strive to satisfy their endless wants using the available scares resources.

Commerce

This is the study of trade and aids to trade. Aids to trade are human activities or services that assist trade to take place such as transport, banking, warehousing and communications.

Accounting

This refers to a systematic way of recording business activities which are used for decision making.

Office Practice

This refers to all the activities that are carried out in an office. For example, communication, filing, reproduction of documents and clerical work.

Entrepreneurship

This is the study of the activities involved in the process of identifying a business opportunity and acquiring the necessary resources to start and run a business.

Importance of business

  1. Assist members of the society to relate the knowledge, skills and attitudes acquired to the day to day running of the business.
  2. Equips the members of the society with skills and knowledge to start and run a business
  3. Assist the individuals with in appreciating the role of business in provision of goods and services.
  4. Makes the members of the society to appreciate the need for good business management practices.
  5. Helps individual to attain self-discipline and positive attitude towards work.
  6. Equips the individual with knowledge and skills to require to evaluate business performance.

Introduction

Purpose of business

The main purpose of starting a business include:

  • To help individuals to interact with other people.
  • To earn foreign exchange.
  • To earn money for your basic needs.
  • To create job opportunities.
  • To enable an individual acquire other goods and services.

Business activities and their effect on business

This refers to conditions or factors that affect business operations. They are divided into two;

  • Internal environment.
  • External environment.

Internal environment/ micro-environment

This consist of factors which are within the business unit itself. These factors include:

  1. Business structure

    2. This is a formal arrangement of activities that are carried out at various levels of the organization so that the objectives of the business can be achieved.

  2. Resources

    3. These are things used to achieve an objective of an organization. They include:

    • Human resource
    • Financial resources
    • Physical resources
    • technology

  3. Business culture

    4. Combinations of employees, expectations, beliefs and values within the business.

  4. Owners

    5. These are people who provided finances to start the business.

External environment/ micro-environment

These are factors that affect the business from outside. These factors include the following factors:

  1. Economic environment

    • Changes in price of other related goods

  2. Demographic environment

    3. This is the population change. Increase in population increases the ability to buy more goods compared to reduction in population. It include the following:

    • Size of the population.
    • Geographical distribution.
    • Age and sex distribution.
    • Birth and death rates.

  3. Legal â€“ political environment

    4. These are laws and policies that regulate business activities. Hence the business to be formed in a country must be in line with the laws and regulations.

  4. Technological environment

  5. Cultural Environment

    6. Culture are the norms that regulate behaviors of people in a society. Culture dictates how people live and what they consume.

    7. This is where firms are trying to outdo each other in their efforts to maximize profits.

Human wants

These are the desires that human beings strive to satisfy by using goods and services.

Satisfaction of human wants

This is the process of acquiring and using goods and services.

Characteristics of human wants

  1. They are complementary

    2. Satisfaction of one want may lead to another want.

  2. Competitive

    3. They compete for attention such that each one of them yearns to be satisfied first.

  3. Vary in urgency and intensity

    4. The amount of goods to be satisfied vary from one person to another and from time to time depending on age, sex and situation.

  4. They are insatiable

    5. Human wants cannot be fully satisfied because they are endless and unlimited in number.

  5. They require resource

    6. Human wants requires resources to be satisfied such as money.

  6. Some are universal

    7. Most human wants are common to everybody although they vary in quantities.

  7. They are recurrent ( repetitive)

    8. A particular want will demand to be satisfied over and over again because wants are not fully satiable.

Goods and services

Goods are physical commodities that are tangible while services are actions which are intangible.

Chacteristics of goods

  1. Are tangible

    2. They can be touched and felt because they are material.

  2. Can be stored

    3. They can be stored for future .The ones that can be stored for longer time are called durable goods while perishable goods cannot be stored for a longer time.

  3. Quality can be standardized

    4. The quality of goods can be made uniform through mechanization.

  4. Can change possession

    5. Goods can change ownership from one person to another through trade.

  5. Can be seen

    6. Most goods can be seen except for a few exceptions such as air.

  6. Can change in quality over time

    7. Some goods may lose value over time such as motorcars and furniture.

Chacteristics of services

  1. Are intangible

    2. They cannot be felt or touched because they are immaterial.

  2. Cannot be stored

    3. Services cannot be stored for future use because they can are consumed as they are provided.

  3. Quality cannot be standardized

    4. Quality cannot be standardized because the quality of services are highly variable over time and from one provider to another.

  4. Inseparable from the provider

    5. Services cannot be separated from the provider as they are provided directly to the consumer.

  5. Cannot be seen

    6. They cannot be seen since they are intangible and immaterial.
